What is Western Dancing?

Texas is rich with country music, so it is no suprise that Austin is home to many country dancers. Strapping on their cowboy boots, many head over their favorite dance hall to two-step till the wee hours in the morning.

Popular Dances

Two-Step

Two-step is generally the dance that comes first to mind when one brings up western dancing. There are different names and versions for this dance, but basic steps are two quick steps and two slow steps. Leader and follower dance counterclock wise around the floor.

Triple-Two Step

This dance is similar to two-step as it is danced around the floor in the counterclock wise manner. Instead of the basic quick step, it is replaced with a shuffle-like movement. This dance can be danced to a large variety of country and country-pop songs and is danced by country-western fans.

Country Waltz

Dance to your favorite country slow song with the country waltz. Danced in triple meter, dancers start out in a closed embrace and move progressively around the dance floor. Depending on leader, he or she can lead the follower to various moves such as turns, spins, and other patterns.

Polka

This high paced dance will defintely get your heart rate up! Lively and energetic, this dance involves some quick feet, spinning, and even some galloping. It is always important to be aware of one's surroundings during this dance and it is not advised to polka on an extremely crowded floor.
